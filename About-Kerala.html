Discover Kerala: God's Own Country

Discover the Essence of Kerala: Journey through its lush landscapes, tranquil backwaters, and golden beaches. Explore the state's rich cultural heritage, from ancient temples and vibrant festivals to traditional art forms. Uncover the stories and traditions that make Kerala a truly enchanting destination.

About Kerala

Kerala Map

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is renowned for its lush landscapes, serene backwaters, and rich cultural heritage. Known as "God's Own Country," it boasts a diverse ecosystem, including verdant hills, pristine beaches, and extensive waterways. Kerala's cultural tapestry is woven with traditional dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, along with vibrant festivals such as Onam. The state is also recognized for its high literacy rate, advanced healthcare system, and progressive social indicators. Kerala's unique cuisine, featuring coconut and spices, reflects its historical trade links with various global cultures.

Vibrant Culture of Kerala

Festivals and Celebrations:

Kerala's festival calendar is a spectacle of color, music, and dance. Onam, the harvest festival, is celebrated with grand feasts, traditional dances like Thiruvathira and Pulikali, and the renowned Vallamkali (boat races). Thrissur Pooram is another highlight, where majestic elephant processions, dazzling fireworks, and traditional drum performances light up the city.

Performing Arts:

The state is renowned for its classical dance forms. Kathakali, with its elaborate costumes and expressive storytelling, and Mohiniyattam, known for its graceful movements, are intrinsic to Kerala’s cultural identity. The traditional theatre form of Koodiyattam, recognized by UNESCO, showcases the depth of Kerala’s performing arts.

Cuisine:

Kerala’s cuisine is a celebration of its cultural richness. With dishes like Sadya (a traditional feast served on a banana leaf), Puttu and Kadala Curry, and Appam with Stew, the culinary delights of Kerala are as diverse as its culture. The use of coconut, spices, and fresh ingredients creates a unique gastronomic experience.

Architecture and Heritage:

From the ancient Temples and Palaces to the traditional Nalukettu (four-sided courtyards), Kerala’s architecture reflects its historical and cultural evolution. The Dutch, Portuguese, and British influences in towns like Fort Kochi and the traditional Kerala houses with their unique architectural styles narrate stories of the past.
